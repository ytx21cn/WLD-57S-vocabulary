beam v.

To smile with joy.

A person who tells lies loudly and arrogantly.

distraught adj.

Mentally deranged.

dupe v.

To deceive someone.

grind v.

To get together.

gyrate v.

To revolve around.

harpoon n.

A barbed spear or javelin used especially in hunting large fish or whales.

hurdle n.

Barrier, obstacle.

jerk n.

An annoyingly foolish person; an unlikeable person.

libel n.

The act of publishing a false statement that causes people to have a bad opinion of someone.

muse n.

A source of inspiration.

phony adj.

Not real; bogus.

pitch v.

To hit upon or happen upon something.

pounce v.

To make a sudden assault or approach.

prick n.

A spiteful or contemptible man often having some authority.

self-effacing adj.

Modest; not trying to get attention to or praise for the knowledge or ability of oneself.

shaky adj.

Lacking in firmness; lacking in authority or reliability.

shred v.

To cut off; to demolish.

snobby adj.

Having or showing the attitude of people who think they are better than others.

solicitous adj.

Concerning or caring for someone else's health, happiness, etc.
